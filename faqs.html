Frequently Asked Questions

To provide quick, clear answers to the most common questions prospective students, parents, and other stakeholders may have about Shajid College of Nursing. The FAQ will reduce repetitive inquiries, improve user experience on the website, and assist in guiding students through the admission and academic processes.

How can I apply to the college?

Application forms can be obtained online through the official college website or physically at the college‚Äôs admissions office during the admission period.

Are there hostel accommodations for students?

Yes, the college provides on-campus hostel facilities for both male and female students, subject to availability.

What is the cost of tuition and other fees?

Tuition and fees vary by program and academic session. A detailed breakdown is provided on the college's website or at the admissions office.

Do students go on clinical postings?

Yes, all students must undergo supervised clinical experiences at approved hospitals and health centres as part of their training.

Can I work while studying?

Due to the demanding nature of the programs, full-time employment is not recommended. However, part-time engagements may be possible with proper time management.

Is there financial aid or scholarship available?

Some scholarships and sponsorships may be available through government initiatives or partner organizations. Students are encouraged to inquire at the bursary or student affairs office.

Where is Shajid College of Nursing and Midwifery located?

The college is located at Wamaba Road, Akwanga, opposite Central Primary School, Akwabga, providing a safe and conducive environment for learning and skill development.

What is the age requirement for admission?

Applicants must be at least 17 years old at the time of admission. There is no upper age limit, but all applicants must be physically and mentally fit for nursing training.

When does the academic session begin?

The academic year typically begins in September. However, applicants are advised to check the college website or admissions office for the exact dates each year.

Are uniforms and textbooks provided?

Students are required to purchase their own uniforms and textbooks. Approved styles and booklists are provided upon admission.

Is there a dress code or conduct policy?

Yes, students must adhere to the college‚Äôs professional dress code and code of conduct, which promotes discipline, cleanliness, and respect within the learning environment.

Can international students apply?

Yes, international applicants are welcome but must present equivalent academic qualifications and meet admission requirements. They must also obtain the necessary study permits.

What facilities are available on campus?

Shajid College has well-equipped lecture halls, skills labs, a library, a computer lab, student hostels, a cafeteria, and access to partner clinical training centers.

Will I receive a license after graduation?

Graduates who pass the Nursing and Midwifery Council of Nigeria (NMCN) licensing examination will be registered and licensed as Registered Nurses (RN) or Registered Midwives (RM).

Are there opportunities for further education after graduation?

Yes, graduates can pursue post-basic specialties, BSc Nursing programs, or international certification depending on career goals and qualifications.

Is there support for job placement after graduation?

While direct job placement is not guaranteed, the college offers career guidance, and many students secure employment through the college‚Äôs reputation and connections with hospitals and clinics.

Can I visit the campus before applying?

Yes, prospective students and their guardians are welcome to visit. Guided tours can be arranged through the admissions office during working hours.
